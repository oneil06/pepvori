Overview

What this protocol
does for you.

The Longevity Protocol uses Sermorelin and Tesamorelin — two of the most clinically studied GHRH analogues — to restore growth hormone levels toward the physiological range of a younger adult. GH declines by roughly 14% per decade after age 30, and this decline underlies many of the hallmarks of biological aging: increased visceral fat, reduced lean mass, declining energy, and deteriorating sleep quality.

Rather than simply slowing the outward signs of aging, this protocol targets the underlying hormonal architecture that drives vitality. It's designed for those in their 40s, 50s, and beyond who want to maintain healthspan — the number of years spent in genuine good health — not just lifespan.

The peptides

What's in

Sermorelin GHRH Analogue

The foundation

Naturally occurring

Sermorelin is a synthetic analogue of the first 29 amino acids of natural GHRH — the endogenous signal that tells the pituitary to release growth hormone. Unlike exogenous HGH, Sermorelin works through the body's own feedback mechanisms, which means GH levels rise in a controlled, physiological way. It's one of the best-studied GHRH peptides for longevity applications, with a long clinical track record in age-related GH decline.

Primary action
GH restoration

Tesamorelin GHRH Analogue

The visceral fat reducer

FDA-studied

Tesamorelin is a stabilised GHRH analogue with a particularly strong evidence base for reducing visceral adipose tissue — the deep abdominal fat strongly associated with metabolic disease and accelerated aging. It works synergistically with Sermorelin, with each peptide activating GH release through slightly different mechanisms for a more comprehensive effect. Tesamorelin's clinical data is some of the most robust in the peptide space.
