Overview

What this protocol
does for you.

The Recovery & Repair protocol is built around three of the most clinically studied peptides for musculoskeletal healing — BPC-157, TB-500, and GHK-Cu. Together, they work synergistically to accelerate tissue repair at the cellular level, reduce the inflammatory signalling that slows healing, and restore structural integrity to tendons, ligaments, and muscle.

Unlike conventional approaches that primarily manage pain, this protocol targets the underlying biological mechanisms of recovery — making it particularly effective for persistent injuries that haven't responded to rest, physio, or other treatments.

The peptides

What's in
this protocol.

BPC-157 Body Protection Compound

The primary healer

Naturally occurring

BPC-157 is a 15-amino acid peptide derived from a protein found in gastric juice. It's one of the most studied peptides for musculoskeletal repair, with a strong safety record and rapid onset. It works by upregulating growth hormone receptors, stimulating angiogenesis (new blood vessel formation), and directly promoting tendon and ligament cell survival.

Primary action
Tendon repair
Onset
1–2 weeks
Route
Subcutaneous
TB-500 Thymosin Beta-4

The tissue mobiliser

Naturally occurring

TB-500 is a synthetic version of Thymosin Beta-4, a protein present in almost all human cells. Its primary role in this protocol is systemic — it promotes cell migration to injury sites, reduces inflammation, increases flexibility in connective tissue, and accelerates healing by modulating actin, a key structural protein in cellular repair. It works particularly well stacked with BPC-157 as each targets different aspects of the repair cascade.

Primary action
Cell migration
Onset

Route
Subcutaneous
GHK-Cu Copper Peptide

The regenerator

Naturally occurring

GHK-Cu is a copper-binding tripeptide that declines significantly with age. In the context of recovery, it plays a crucial role in stimulating collagen synthesis, promoting wound healing, and activating the body's antioxidant systems to protect tissue from inflammatory damage. It rounds out the protocol by supporting the structural rebuild phase that follows initial repair.

Primary action
Collagen synthesis

What to expect

Your recovery
timeline.

1
Week 1–2
Initial inflammation reduction

Most patients notice a reduction in acute pain and swelling within the first two weeks. Sleep quality often improves as the body's inflammatory burden decreases. This is the foundation phase — the peptides are beginning to modulate the inflammatory cascade and increase local blood flow to injured tissue.

2
Week 3–4
Tissue repair accelerates

Measurable improvement in range of motion and functional strength typically becomes evident. Patients with tendon or ligament injuries often report a meaningful reduction in pain with activity. Your physician will conduct a check-in around week 4 to assess progress and adjust dosing if needed.

3
Week 5–8
Structural rebuild & consolidation

GHK-Cu's collagen-stimulating effects come fully online. Training loads can typically be reintroduced progressively. For persistent injuries that haven't healed in months or years, this phase often marks a genuine turning point. Many patients describe returning to activities they'd written off entirely.

4
Month 3+
Maintenance & optimisation

Depending on your recovery goals, your physician may recommend cycling off the protocol, reducing frequency, or transitioning to a maintenance dose. Some patients choose to layer in a second protocol at this stage — for example, adding Growth & Strength once the recovery baseline is established.
